Introduction OF BMWM

As per the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 and the Bio-Medical Waste (Management and Handling) Rules notified in July 1998, it is the responsibility of every “occupier” (in the case of TSRMMCHRC, the Chairman/Managing Trustee) to ensure that biomedical waste is managed in a manner that causes no harm to human health or the environment.

TSRMMCHRC follows the Bio-Medical Waste Management Rules, 2016, notified by the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India on 28th March 2016. Currently, biomedical waste management at TSRMMCHRC is outsourced to a Common Bio-Medical Waste Treatment Facility, M/s Medicare Enviro Systems Pvt. Ltd.

The BMW Management Rules, 2016 have expanded their scope to include activities such as vaccination, blood donation, and surgical camps. Waste is now classified into four categories to improve segregation at source and must be handled as per Schedule I. Certain wastes require on-site pre-treatment through disinfection or sterilization, and untreated waste cannot be stored beyond 48 hours.

The rules mandate barcoding of waste bags, training and immunization of healthcare workers, reporting of major accidents, phasing out chlorinated plastics, stricter incineration standards, and emission limits for dioxins and furans. Authorization procedures have been simplified, and the use of common treatment facilities is encouraged. State Governments are required to support implementation through advisory committees and infrastructure provision.
